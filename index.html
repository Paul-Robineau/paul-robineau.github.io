Resume

  • Nanos & Environment Researcher


    A master in Optics and Nanotechnology allowed me to have a general overview on Nanosciences, from theorethical frameworks to current state of applications in different areas. Following this by a PhD in the area of Nanobiophotonics let me develop specific competencies at the interface of Nanosciences, Biology, and Optics.


    Currently working as postdoctoral researcher at the Hubert Curien Pluridisciplinary Institute (IPHC), in the context of CO2-InnO Interreg European project. Goal : conducting prospective Life-Cycle Assessment (LCA) studies regarding the Fessenheim NPP decommissioning, the recycling of VLL (very low-level) radioactive metals, and a micro-CHP demonstrator coupled to H2 production through electrolysis.


    My interest in Social Sciences and Humanities led me to gain knowledge on the varied considerations on the intertwined relations between the research field and the civil, industrial, and political ones. I am currently working on the organization of a scientific convention dedicated to these subjects (URL), and writing a research program aiming at a synergy between the fields of sustainability and nanosciences.


  • R&D Engineer


    My engineer degree in Materials: Technology & Economy from the University of Technology of Troyes (UTT) allowed me to work in R&D during 2 Internships and pursue a PhD Thesis. My first professionnal experiences, in Industrial Research at CEA Tech, gave me the skills to be autonomous on many areas and to direct my research with the short and long term technical feasability always in mind.


    From theorethical knowledge of Structural properties of Matter and Light-Matter interactions, to practical skills in Life-Cycle Assessment and Eco-conception, Materials for Energy or for Optoelectronics, adding proficient use of varied software tools (Microsoft Office, Matlab, Igor Pro, Bash, Adobe Creative Suite), and critical insights from Sociology of Organizations, History & Philosphy of Sciences, I have the profile of a reflexive engineer with systemic and interdisciplinary mindset.


  • Physics & Ecology of Materials Teacher


    Beside my research activities, I had the pleasure to oversee pratical works in physics & chemistry as a teacher assistant in 2020-2021 at the University of Technology of Troyes (UTT).


    I also designed and was responsible to teach a section on abiotic resources inside a general course on modeling Human-Systems-Nature interactions. We studied it from A to Z, looking at current consumption & production, and their future – using for that a sociotechnical perspective informed by social & environmental sciences. Slides available here. I also helped students adopt a systemic approach to their modeling with an introduction to its conceptual basis and methodology. Slides available here


  • Project Manager


    There is very few research that does not need collaboration and work organization. From group projects during courses at the university, to associative events or first-aid service, my skills in project & team organization have been developped in varied contexts.


    These experiences gave me the opportunity to create & conduct planning, to work with and sometimes manage teams from 3 to 15 people. On the software side, I use daily several tools like Slack/Discord/Miro, GitHub, or Trello.
